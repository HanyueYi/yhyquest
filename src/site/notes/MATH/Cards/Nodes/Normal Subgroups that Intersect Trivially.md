> [!proposition]
> Let $H$ and $K$ be normal subgroups of a group $G$ such that they intersect trivially. Then $hk=kh$ for any $h\in H$ and $k\in K$.

`\begin{proof}`
Note that $hkh^{-1}k^{-1}\in H\cap K=\{1\}$.
`\end{proof}`
