> [!proposition]
> Let $G$ be a $2$-group. If $\exp(G)=2$, then $G$ is abelian.

`\begin{proof}`
For any $1\neq x,y\in G$, since $x=x^{-1}$ and $y=y^{-1}$, we have that 

$$[x,y]=xyx^{-1}y^{-1}=(xy)(xy)=1$$

and so $G$ is abelian.
`\end{proof}`
